Hookup Order

  1. 1Secure the loose XT30 pigtail leads in Wagos before plugging in the battery.
  2. 2Put pigtail red, servo red, and buck IN+ into the positive Wago.
  3. 3Put pigtail black, servo ground, and buck IN- into the negative Wago.
  4. 4Set buck OUT+ to OUT- at 5.0V before powering the XIAO from it.
  5. 5Patch XIAO PWM to a breadboard row, then jumper that row to the servo signal wire.

Hard Rules

No raw 2S into XIAO. Use USB-C or the buck converter set to 5.0V. No servo power through breadboard. Servo red/black stay on the Wago battery path. PWM through breadboard is OK. The orange/yellow signal wire is low-current control wiring. Grounds must meet. Servo ground, buck ground, and XIAO GND share the same reference.

Desk-test assumption: the servo can spike several amps during startup or stall. The XIAO provides only the control signal through the breadboard, so the high-current wiring stays on the XT30 pigtail and Wago connectors.
